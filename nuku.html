What is Nuku Collective?

“A collective based in Ōtepoti, Aotearoa deciated to showcasing local creatives through an online community, aiming to foster connections, collaborations and conversations.”

For us the meaning of nuku represents our fluidity in form and function, we aim to create new spaces and new ways of interacting with creatives and their content. Nuku also meaning ‘the earth’ ties in with our strong values of Kaitiakitanga and treating Papatūānuku with aroha.

Nuku Collective was started by two friends/Design students in their third year at Otago Polytechnic. Sofia Heke (Fashion Design) & Zoe Hart (Communication Design), soon to be graduates and active members of Aotearoa's design community they saw a need for an accessible, positive, diverse community of creatives to share, showcase, converse and connect. Nuku was founded on the idea of collaboration and we see every featured creative as a collaborator of ours.

Nuku Collective acknowledges and respects ngā iwi Māori as the Tangata Whenua of Aotearoa and are committed to upholding the principles of the Treaty of Waitangi.
