Kia ora! My name is Bella and I’m an Ōtepoti based graphic designer and illustrator! I’m an artist at heart, and creativity is my greatest tool for communication and self-expression. Through my design practice, I aim to create subversive and thought-provoking work. My designs are quite often informed by my experience with genderqueer identity and sexuality. I believe design is one of the greatest tools for breaking down social barriers and changing the way we think!

I started Enby Collective as my major graduate project in my design communication degree. I wanted to create a space for trans and gender-queer creatives to be celebrated, heard and uplifted.

The idea for Enby came about in a discussion between myself and my flatmate who is also trans. We often have discussions about how as genderqueer people we spend a lot of our time and energy educating people on why they should merely respect our existence.

I’ve always felt a real lack of compassion and empathy from the world - people feel as if they have to understand us in order to say we shouldn’t be experiencing violence or discrimination. So much of trans-liberation has been about educating others, but we need spaces to feel appreciated, loved and celebrated.

I designed and curated a publication that serves as an anthology of trans identity, fashion, art and culture at the time each issue is made. I wanted to create something that would excite the community and bring trans creatives together. I also really just wanted it to be something that would comfort those who are struggling with navigating identity.

I never really had something like this when I first started navigating my identity, so I want to provide that for others. Amazing trans creatives from across the country contributed their art, experiences, poetry and more to the publication. It’s honestly been such a beautiful and affirming experience for me personally and I’m so excited to share it with my community!
